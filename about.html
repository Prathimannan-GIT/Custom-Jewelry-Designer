Our Heritage

Three generations of excellence

From a small family workshop to a global atelier, our journey spans over 75 years of unparalleled craftsmanship and innovation.

Timeline

A legacy of innovation

Our journey through decades of excellence, from humble beginnings to global recognition.

The Foundation (1948-1970)

Marcus Blackwood established our first workshop in Manhattan's Diamond District, specializing in hand-engraved signet rings and custom family crests for New York's elite families.

Global Expansion (1971-1995)

Under Eleanor Blackwood's leadership, we expanded internationally, opening ateliers in London's Mayfair and Paris' Place Vendôme, serving European royalty and aristocracy.

Modern Renaissance (1996-2020)

James Blackwood revolutionized our craft by integrating CAD technology while preserving traditional techniques, earning us the first of many international design awards.

Contemporary Excellence (2021-Present)

Today, under Sophia Blackwood-Chen, we lead the industry in sustainable luxury, blockchain provenance, and innovative jewelry technology while honoring our heritage.

Traditions

Time-honored techniques

Our master artisans preserve centuries-old techniques while embracing innovation to create truly exceptional pieces.

The Blackwood Method

Our proprietary engraving technique developed in 1952, featuring microscopic precision that creates depth and dimension impossible to replicate with modern machinery.

Heritage Alloy Formulation

Secret metal recipes passed down through generations, creating unique rose and white gold formulations that resist tarnishing and maintain luster for decades.

Artisanal Stone Selection

Our gemologists personally visit mines worldwide, selecting stones based on our family's proprietary grading system that exceeds industry standards.

Recognition

Industry leadership and honors

Our commitment to excellence has earned us the highest accolades in the luxury jewelry world.

Prestigious Awards

Recipient of the Queen's Award for Enterprise (UK), the French Legion of Honor for Craftsmanship, and the American Gem Society's Triple Zero Award for perfect diamond cutting.

Academic Partnerships

Collaborative research programs with GIA, Gemological Institute of America, and the Royal College of Art, advancing jewelry science and design education.

Published Works

Our family has authored five definitive texts on jewelry craftsmanship, including "The Art of Micro-Engraving" and "Sustainable Luxury in Modern Jewelry Design."

Diplomatic Relations

Official jeweler to multiple royal houses and governments, creating ceremonial pieces and diplomatic gifts that represent cultural heritage and international relations.
